WHY PEOPLE CARE ABOUT THE UNIVERSE

The universe is an endless field of exploration that astronomers explore. Striving to understand the nature of space, scientists investigate the structure of the universe by observing celestial bodies. With the help of telescopes and space probes, they study distant galaxies and black holes. They work hard to understand the origin and evolution of the universe. While the big bang theory explains the beginning of the universe, deeper secrets still await unraveling. Scientists dealing with the universe offer critical clues about the existence of humanity and our place in the universe. The infinity and mysteries of the universe is a magical field that constantly excites the scientific world and continues to research.

WHAT ARE BLACK HOLES

Black holes are among the most mysterious and striking phenomena of the universe. It is the region where giant stars collapse after completing their lives. In these regions where gravity is extraordinarily strong, even matter and light cannot escape. Black holes bend the space around them, slowing down time, and bending light to create stunning images of varying sizes. The concept of "singularity", called the "point of infinite density" at their center, is beyond the laws of physics. As astronomers and astrophysicists try to understand black holes with space telescopes and other research instruments, they try to unravel the secrets of these mysterious and magical structures that exist in the universe. Black holes represent a magnificent but also surprising and complex aspect of the universe that constantly astounds scientists.

ABOUT THE INFINITY OF THE UNIVERSE

The infinity of the universe is a subject that humanity has wondered and discussed for centuries. Although it is difficult to even understand how big the universe is, there is no definitive answer as to whether it is infinite or not. While there are cosmologists who believe that the universe is spread out in unlimited space, according to other theories, the universe may have a limit. While the Big Bang theory suggests that the universe started at a point and is constantly expanding, it is not yet known whether this expansion takes place in an infinite space. This obscurity is a constant source of inspiration for scientists and researchers to understand the mysterious and magical nature of the universe. Discussions about the infinity of the universe turn into a fascinating journey of discovery that questions the meaning of humanity's existence and our place in the universe.

BLACK HOLE

Black holes are one of the most mysterious and intriguing phenomena in space. These are regions with very high density and extraordinarily strong gravitational force. Black holes usually appear during the evolution of stars.

WHAT ARE BLACK HOLES

There are three basic types of black holes: Gravitational Center: Small black holes with a mass between 3 and 20 times the mass of the sun, formed by the collapse of a star. Medium Black Holes: Such black holes, with masses millions of times the mass of the sun, can be found in the centers of galaxies. Supermassive Black Holes: Gigantic black holes with masses reaching billions of solar masses. They can be found in the centers of galaxies or in large galaxy clusters.

BLACK HOLE EXAMPLE

LMC X-1 is a binary star system located in the Large Magellanic Cloud. The primary star has a large mass and turned into a neutron star or black hole in the final phase of its life cycle. The secondary star is a smaller star and orbits the primary star, which is a black hole.

New feature

December 14, 2020 by Chris

In the pursuit of understanding the cosmos, one of the most intriguing enigmas that continues to baffle scientists is the enigmatic phenomenon known as "dark matter." This mysterious substance, which comprises a significant portion of the universe, remains invisible and undetectable by conventional means. Despite its elusiveness, the presence of dark matter can be inferred through its gravitational effects on visible matter and light.

  • Vera Rubin: American astronomer Vera Rubin is known for her important work on dark matter. By examining the rotational tendencies of galaxies, he presented evidence of the phenomenon of dark matter.
  • George Smoot: American astrophysicist George Smoot won the 2006 Nobel Prize in Physics for her research on dark matter and discovering anisotropies for the early universe.
  • Reinhard Genzel and Andrea Ghez: German astrophysicist Reinhard Genzel and American astronomer Andrea Ghez made important observations on dark matter by examining the orbits of stars around the supermassive black hole at the center of the Milky Way and won the 2020 Nobel Prize in Physics.

These scientists are trying to solve one of the biggest mysteries of the universe with their research on dark matter.
